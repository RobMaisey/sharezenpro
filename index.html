The problem

Your toys deserve better than a dusty corner.

A cabin, boat, or plane is one of the biggest things you'll ever own — and it sits idle most of the year. Share it informally and the scheduling clashes, fuzzy costs, and forgotten maintenance start to wear on the people you love.

ShareZen takes the friction out of sharing something valuable with the people you trust — so the asset gets used, stays cared for, and brings you closer instead of driving you apart.

How it works

Three things to keep straight. One place to do it.

Sharing isn't renting. ShareZen manages the three layers a rental marketplace never has to — for a group that already shares a stake.

Layer 1

Who owns it

A single primary owner, or fractional and quarter-share co-owners — equity stays clear, however title is held.

Layer 2

Who pays to run it

Slip fees, insurance, fuel, maintenance — shared fairly, even when the cost-sharers aren't the equity-holders.

Layer 3

Who uses it, and when

Invite, share, or even rent to a friend on one calendar. No double-bookings, no awkward texts.

Sharing ≠ renting

A trusted circle, not an anonymous market.

The line isn't rent versus share — it's trusted people versus anonymous strangers. Stay on the right side of it and everything changes.

✦ A trusted circle

  • It stays a home, not inventory — leave the skis in the garage and the photo on the wall.
  • Cared for by people who actually respect the place.
  • Keeps towns and communities genuinely lived-in, season after season.

✕ An anonymous market

  • Strip the soul out and run it as a sterile commercial unit.
  • Wear, churn, and strangers who'll never love it like you do.
  • Off-season ghost towns of empty investment units.

"Leave the skis in the garage and the family photo on the wall." — the difference between sharing and renting

Uber, Airbnb, and Lime were branded as the "sharing economy." They're anonymous rental platforms — and in many places they displaced real sharing instead of scaling it. ShareZen is built for the other side of that line.

Questions

Good to know.

Is this like Airbnb?
No. ShareZen is for sharing among people you trust — co-owners, family, friends — not for listing your asset to strangers. That's the whole point.
Can I rent it to someone?
Yes — to a friend, within your circle, as a way to keep the asset used and cared for. ShareZen just isn't a public rental marketplace.
Who pays for the subscription?
The asset owner. In most cases one subscription covers the whole partnership, club, or group.
What does it cost, and is there a trial?
$19/month or $204/year, with a full 30-day free trial for new customers. Cancel anytime.
Which assets does it work for?
Vacation homes and cabins, boats, light aircraft, and RVs — anything valuable that a trusted group shares.
Why is ShareZen a subscription now?
It ran free for years. A simple subscription keeps the platform stable and funds ongoing development — no ads, no selling your data.
